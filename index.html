Renewable Energy Education

Learn about different types of renewable energy sources and their benefits.

Solar Energy

Harnesses the sun's power using photovoltaic cells and solar thermal systems.

Solar Energy

Advantages:
  • Abundant Resource
  • Environmental Benefits
  • Low Operational Costs
Disadvantages:
  • Intermittent Supply
  • High Initial Costs
  • Space Requirements
Applications:
  • Residential
  • Commercial
  • Utility-Scale
  • Off-Grid

Wind Energy

Converts the kinetic energy of wind into mechanical power and electricity.

Wind Energy

Advantages:
  • Renewable Source
  • Low Operational Costs
  • Scalability
Disadvantages:
  • Variable Output
  • Noise and Visual Impact
  • Wildlife Impact
Applications:
  • Onshore Wind Farms
  • Offshore Wind Farms
  • Small Wind Systems

Hydroelectric Power

Generates electricity by harnessing the energy of moving water.

Hydroelectric Power

Advantages:
  • Reliable and Consistent
  • Low Emissions
  • Secondary Benefits
Disadvantages:
  • Environmental Impact
  • High Initial Costs
  • Limited Locations
Applications:
  • Large-Scale Hydropower
  • Small-Scale Hydropower
  • Pumped Storage

Geothermal Energy

Utilizes heat from the Earth's interior to generate power.

Geothermal Energy

Advantages:
  • Consistent Power Supply
  • Low Emissions
  • Small Footprint
Disadvantages:
  • Geographical Limitations
  • High Initial Costs
  • Potential for Induced Seismicity
Applications:
  • Electricity Generation
  • Direct Use Applications
  • Geothermal Heat Pumps

Biofuel

Produced from organic materials, including plant materials and animal waste.

Biofuel

Advantages:
  • Carbon Neutral
  • Waste Utilization
  • Domestic Production
Disadvantages:
  • Land Use
  • Energy Intensive
  • Variable Emissions
Applications:
  • Transportation
  • Heating
  • Electricity Generation

Electric Vehicles (EVs)

Electric vehicles are a great way to reduce your carbon footprint. They offer many benefits including:

  • Zero emissions
  • Lower operating costs
  • Reduced noise pollution
  • Government incentives
