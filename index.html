Renewable Energy Education

Learn about different types of renewable energy sources and their benefits.

Solar Energy

Harnesses the sun's power using photovoltaic cells and solar thermal systems.

Advantages:

  • Abundant Resource
  • Environmental Benefits
  • Low Operational Costs

Disadvantages:

  • Intermittent Supply
  • High Initial Costs
  • Space Requirements

Applications:

  • Residential
  • Commercial
  • Utility-Scale
  • Off-Grid

Wind Energy

Converts the kinetic energy of wind into mechanical power and electricity.

Advantages:

  • Renewable Source
  • Low Operational Costs
  • Scalability

Disadvantages:

  • Variable Output
  • Noise and Visual Impact
  • Wildlife Impact

Applications:

  • Onshore Wind Farms
  • Offshore Wind Farms
  • Small Wind Systems

Hydroelectric Power

Generates electricity by harnessing the energy of moving water.

Advantages:

  • Reliable and Consistent
  • Low Emissions
  • Secondary Benefits

Disadvantages:

  • Environmental Impact
  • High Initial Costs
  • Limited Locations

Applications:

  • Large-Scale Hydropower
  • Small-Scale Hydropower
  • Pumped Storage

Geothermal Energy

Utilizes heat from the Earth's interior to generate power.

Advantages:

  • Consistent Power Supply
  • Low Emissions
  • Small Footprint

Disadvantages:

  • Geographical Limitations
  • High Initial Costs
  • Potential for Induced Seismicity

Applications:

  • Electricity Generation
  • Direct Use Applications
  • Geothermal Heat Pumps

Biofuel

Produced from organic materials, including plant materials and animal waste.

Advantages:

  • Carbon Neutral
  • Waste Utilization
  • Domestic Production

Disadvantages:

  • Land Use
  • Energy Intensive
  • Variable Emissions

Applications:

  • Transportation
  • Heating
  • Electricity Generation
